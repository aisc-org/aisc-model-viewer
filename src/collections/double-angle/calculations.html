This page demonstrates calculations to:

  • determine the design strength of the double angle tension member and bolted connection
  • assess the compliance of the connection with geometric limitations

The calculations are based on the 2016 AISC Specification and the 15th Edition AISC Manaul.

Material and Geometric Properties

As indicated on the two-dimensional structural drawing, both the double angle tension member and the gusset plate conform to A36.

From AISC Manual Table 2-4, the material properties are as follows:

ASTM A36
\(F_y = 36 \text{ ksi}\)
\(F_u = 58 \text{ ksi}\)

From AISC Manual Table 1-7, the geometric properties are as follows:

L6x6x5/8
\(A_g = 7.13 \text{ in.}^2\)
\(\bar{x} = 1.72 \text{ in.}\)

Tensile Yielding

Calculate the nominal strength using AISC Specification Equation D2-1.

\[ \begin{align} P_n &= F_y A_g \\ &= (36 \text{ ksi})(2 \times 7.13 \text{ in.}^2) \\ &= 513 \text{ kips} \end{align} \]

Calculate the design strength by applying the resistance factor.

\[ \phi P_n = 0.9(513 \text{ kips}) = 462 \text{ kips}\]

Tensile Rupture

A depiction of tensile rupture failure for this connection is shown in this three-dimensional view

The net area, \(A_n\), is shown in red. It is equal to the gross area minus the material removed for two bolt holes:

\[ \begin{align} A_n &= A_g - 2(d_h + \tfrac{1}{16} \text{ in.})t \\ &= (2 \times 7.13 \text{ in.}^2) - 2(\tfrac{15}{16} \text{ in.} + \tfrac{1}{16} \text{ in.})(\tfrac{5}{8} \text{ in.}) \\ &= 13.01 \text{ in.}^2 \end{align} \]

From the description of the element in AISC Specification Table D3.1 Case 8, calculate the shear lag factor, U, as the larger of the values from Case 2 and Case 8.

Case 2, where \(l\) is the length of connection from the centerline of the first bolt to centerline of the last bolt.

\[ \begin{align} U &= 1 - \frac{\bar{x}}{l} \\ &= 1 - \frac{1.72 \text{ in.}}{24 \text{ in.}} \\ &= 0.928 \end{align} \]

Case 8.

\[ U = 0.8 \]

Therefore, use the larger \(U = 0.928\).

Calculate the effective net area using AISC Specification Equation D3-1.

\[ \begin{align} A_e &= A_n U \\ &= (13.01 \text{ in.}^2)(0.928) \\ &= 12.07 \text{ in.}^2 \end{align} \]

Calculate the nominal strength using AISC Specification Equation D2-2.

\[ \begin{align} P_n &= F_u A_e \\ &= (58 \text{ ksi})(12.07 \text{ in.}^2) \\ &= 700 \text{ kips} \end{align} \]

Calculate the design strength by applying the resistance factor.

\[ \phi P_n = 0.75(700 \text{ kips}) = 525 \text{ kips}\]

Block Shear Rupture

A depiction of block shear rupture failure for this connection is shown in this three-dimensional view

The only valid and plausible block shear rupture failure path is shown below

Calculate the gross area subject to shear.

\[ \begin{align} A_{gv} &= 2 \times (26\tfrac{1}{8} \text{ in.})(\tfrac{5}{8} \text{ in.}) \\ &= 32.73 \text{ in.}^2 \end{align} \]

Calculate the net area subject to shear as gross area subject to shear minus the material removed for eight and a half bolt holes on each angle.

\[ \begin{align} A_{nv} &= A_{gv} - 2 \times 8\tfrac{1}{2} \times (d_h + \tfrac{1}{16} \text{ in.})t \\ &= 32.73 \text{ in.}^2 - 2 \times 8\tfrac{1}{2} \times (\tfrac{15}{16} \text{ in.} + \tfrac{1}{16} \text{ in.})(\tfrac{5}{8} \text{ in.}) \\ &= 22.11 \text{ in.}^2 \end{align} \]

Calculate the gross area subject to tension.

\[ \begin{align} A_{gt} &= 2 \times (3\tfrac{3}{4} \text{ in.})(\tfrac{5}{8} \text{ in.}) \\ &= 4.69 \text{ in.}^2 \end{align} \]

Calculate the net area subject to tension as gross area subject to tension minus the material removed for one half bolt hole on each angle.

\[ \begin{align} A_{nt} &= A_{gt} - 2 \times \tfrac{1}{2} \times (d_h + \tfrac{1}{16} \text{ in.})t \\ &= 4.69 \text{ in.}^2 - 2 \times \tfrac{1}{2} \times (\tfrac{15}{16} \text{ in.} + \tfrac{1}{16} \text{ in.})(\tfrac{5}{8} \text{ in.}) \\ &= 4.07 \text{ in.}^2 \end{align} \]

Calculate the nominal strength using AISC Specification Equation J4-5. Take \(U_{bs} = 1.0\)

\[ \begin{align} P_n &= 0.6 F_u A_{nv} + U_{bs} F_u A_{nt} \leq 0.6 F_y A_{gv} + U_{bs} F_u A_{nt} \\ &= 0.6 (58 \text{ ksi}) (22.11 \text{ in.}^2) + (1.0)(58 \text{ ksi}) (4.07 \text{ in.}^2)\\ &\;\;\;\leq 0.6 (36 \text{ ksi}) (32.73 \text{ in.}^2) + (1.0)(58 \text{ ksi}) (4.07 \text{ in.}^2)\\ &= 1005 \text{ kips} \leq 943 \text{ kips} \\ &= 943 \text{ kips} \end{align} \]

Calculate the design strength by applying the resistance factor.

\[ \phi P_n = 0.75(943 \text{ kips}) = 707 \text{ kips}\]

Bolt Group Strength

The limit states of bolt shear rupture, bearing, and tearout are evaluated together to determine the design strength of the bolt group.

For each bolt, an effective strength is calculated as the lowest strength from the limit states of bolt shear rupture, bearing, and tearout.

From AISC Specification Table J3.2, Group A bolts, when threads are not excluded from shear planes:

\(F_{nv} = 54 \text{ ksi}=\)

Calculate the nominal unthreaded body area of of bolt.

\[ \begin{align} A_b &= \tfrac{\pi}{4} d^2 \\ &= \tfrac{\pi}{4}(\tfrac{7}{8} \text{ in.})^2 \\ &= 0.601 \text{ in.}^2 \end{align} \]

Bolt #1

The nominal shear strength of for the nominal strength for the limit state of bolt shear rupture using AISC Specification Equation J3-1.

Calculate the nominal strength for the limit state of bolt shear rupture using AISC Specification Equation J3-1. Note that the bolts are in double shear.

\[ \begin{align} r_n &= F_{nv} A_b \\ &= (54 \text{ ksi})(2 \times 0.601 \text{ in.}^2) \\ &= 64.9 \text{ kips} \end{align} \]

Calculate the nominal strength for the limit state of bearing in the double angle using AISC Specification Equation J3-6a.

\[ \begin{align} r_n &= 2.4dtF_u \\ &= 2.4(\tfrac{7}{8} \text{ in.})(2 \times \tfrac{5}{8} \text{ in.})(58 \text{ ksi}) \\ &= 152.3 \text{ kips} \end{align} \]

Calculate the nominal strength for the limit state of bearing in the gusset plate using AISC Specification Equation J3-6a.

\[ \begin{align} r_n &= 2.4dtF_u \\ &= 2.4(\tfrac{7}{8} \text{ in.})(\tfrac{5}{8} \text{ in.})(58 \text{ ksi}) \\ &= 76.1 \text{ kips} \end{align} \]

Calculate the nominal strength for the limit state of tearout in the double angle using AISC Specification Equation J3-6c.

\[ \begin{align} l_c &= 2\tfrac{1}{8} \text{ in.} - \tfrac{1}{2}d_h \\ &= 2\tfrac{1}{8} \text{ in.} - \tfrac{1}{2}(\tfrac{15}{16} \text{ in.}) \\ &= 1.656 \text{ in.} \end{align} \]

\[ \begin{align} r_n &= 1.2l_ctF_u \\ &= 1.2(1.656 \text{ in.})(2 \times \tfrac{5}{8} \text{ in.})(58 \text{ ksi}) \\ &= 144.1 \text{ kips} \end{align} \]

Calculate the nominal strength for the limit state of tearout in the gusset plate using AISC Specification Equation J3-6c.

\[ \begin{align} l_c &= 3 \text{ in.} - d_h \\ &= 3 \text{ in.} - \tfrac{15}{16} \text{ in.} \\ &= 2.063 \text{ in.} \end{align} \]

\[ \begin{align} r_n &= 1.2l_ctF_u \\ &= 1.2(2.063 \text{ in.})(\tfrac{5}{8} \text{ in.})(58 \text{ ksi}) \\ &= 74.8 \text{ kips} \end{align} \]

Bolt shear rupture controls. The nominal effective strength of bolt #1 is 64.9 kips.

Bolts #2-8

The effective strength of bolts #2 through #8 is calculated in the same manner, except with different clear distances. Bolt shear rupture controls. The nominal effective strength of bolts #2 through #8 is 64.9 kips.

Bolts #9

The effective strength of bolt #9 is calculated in the same manner, except with different clear distances. Bolt shear rupture controls. The nominal effective strength of bolts #2 through #8 is 64.9 kips.

Bolt group

Calculate the nominal effective strength of the bolt group by summing the nominal effective strength of each bolt.

\[ \begin{align} R_n &= 64.9 \text{ kips} + 7 \times 64.9 \text{ kips} + 64.9 \text{ kips} \\ &= 548 \text{ kips} \end{align} \]

Calculate the design strength by applying the resistance factor.

\[ \phi P_n = 0.75(548 \text{ kips}) = 411 \text{ kips}\]

Bolt Spacing

From Section J3.3 of the AISC Specification, the minimum spacing between bolts is \(2\tfrac{2}{3}d\).

\[ \begin{align} s_{min} &= 2\tfrac{2}{3}d \\ &= 2\tfrac{2}{3}(\tfrac{7}{8} \text{ in.}) \\ &= 2.33 \text{ in.} \end{align} \]

From the User Note in Section J3.3 of the AISC Specification, the preferred minimum spacing between bolts is \(3d\).

\[ \begin{align} s_{min} &= 3d \\ &= 3(\tfrac{7}{8} \text{ in.}) \\ &= 2.63 \text{ in.} \end{align} \]

From Section J3.5 of the AISC Specification, the maximum spacing between bolts is the lesser of 24 times the thickness of this thinner part jointed and 12 in.

\[ \begin{align} s_{max} &= min(24t_{min},12 \text{ in.}) \\ &= min(24(\tfrac{5}{8} \text{ in.}),12 \text{ in.}) \\ &= 12 \text{ in.} \end{align} \]

The provided spacing between bolts is 3 in. which satisfies the required minimum spacing, preferred minimum spacing, and the maximum spacing.

Bolt Edge Distances

From Section J3.4 and Table J3.4 of the AISC Specification, the minimum edge distance for a 7/8 in. diameter bolt is a standard hole is \(1\tfrac{1}{8} \text{ in.}\).

The minimum provided edge distance is \(2\tfrac{1}{8} \text{ in.}\) which satisfies the minimum edge distance requirement.

From Section J3.5 of the AISC Specification, the maximum spacing between bolts is the lesser of 12 times the thickness of the connected part 6 in.

\[ \begin{align} L_{e,max} &= min(12t_{min},6 \text{ in.}) \\ &= min(12(\tfrac{5}{8} \text{ in.}),6 \text{ in.}) \\ &= 6 \text{ in.} \end{align} \]

The maximum provided edge distance is \(3\tfrac{3}{4} \text{ in.}\) which satisfies the maximum edge distance requirement.

Entering and Tightning Clearances

From Table 7-15 of the AISC Manual, the minimum distance from the center of a 7/8 in. diameter A325 bolt and the face of a vertical obstruction is

\(1\tfrac{1}{4} \text{ in.}\)

The distance between the center of the bolts and the face of the outstanding leg of the angles is

\(2\tfrac{1}{4} \text{ in.} - t = 2\tfrac{1}{4} \text{ in.} - \tfrac{5}{8} \text{ in.} = 1\tfrac{5}{8} \text{ in.}\)

The provided clearance exceeds the minimum required clearance, therefore the outstanding leg of the angle will not interfere with the installation of the bolts.
